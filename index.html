üìä Financial Model Overview

This financial model projects operational costs and revenue for Alteram Lifestyle over a 24-month period, divided into three distinct phases of business development. The model incorporates South African market conditions, regulatory requirements, and fintech industry best practices.

Business Model Summary

  • KhulaPay: Lay-by service with platform fees
  • SharePay: Social lending with service fees
  • EasyNow: Buy-now-pay-later with interest revenue
  • FlexLine: Credit line with interest revenue

üöÄ Growth & Volume Parameters

Quarterly Growth Rate (%)

Definition: Expected quarterly growth rate for transaction volume.

Business Impact: Higher rates mean faster business expansion but may require more resources and capital.

Typical Range: 10-25% quarterly (40-100% annually)

Considerations: Balance growth ambition with operational capacity and funding requirements.

Starting Monthly Transactions

Definition: Number of transactions expected in the first month of operations.

Business Impact: Sets the baseline volume for all subsequent calculations and growth projections.

Typical Range: 50-200 transactions/month for new fintech startups

Considerations: Based on market research, pilot programs, or conservative estimates.

Average Order Value (ZAR)

Definition: Average value of each customer transaction in South African Rand.

Business Impact: Primary driver of revenue per transaction and overall business scale.

Typical Range: R1,000 - R10,000 depending on product mix

Considerations: Varies significantly between lay-by (lower) and credit products (higher).

Customer Service: Customers per FTE

Definition: How many customers one full-time customer service employee can handle effectively.

Business Impact: Determines staffing requirements and operational efficiency.

Typical Range: 300-800 customers per FTE

Considerations: Higher numbers indicate better processes and automation.

üí∞ Revenue Parameters

KhulaPay Platform Fee (%)

Definition: Platform fee percentage charged on KhulaPay lay-by transactions.

Revenue Model: Transaction-based fee on lay-by purchases.

Typical Range: 2-5% of transaction value

Business Logic: Lower risk product, so fees are typically lower than credit products.

SharePay Service Fee (%)

Definition: Service fee percentage charged on SharePay social lending transactions.

Revenue Model: Fee-based service for social lending facilitation.

Typical Range: 1.5-3% of transaction value

Business Logic: Social lending has moderate risk, fees reflect service value.

EasyNow Interest Rate (% annual)

Definition: Annual interest rate charged on EasyNow buy-now-pay-later loans.

Revenue Model: Interest income on outstanding loan balances.

Typical Range: 18-36% annually (regulated by NCR)

Business Logic: BNPL products carry higher risk, requiring higher interest rates.

FlexLine Interest Rate (% annual)

Definition: Annual interest rate charged on FlexLine credit line products.

Revenue Model: Interest income on revolving credit balances.

Typical Range: 16-32% annually (regulated by NCR)

Business Logic: Credit lines have highest risk profile, highest interest rates.

üßÆ Revenue Calculation Explained

Monthly Revenue = Monthly Transactions √ó Average Order Value

How It Works:

  1. Starting Point: You begin with a baseline number of transactions (e.g., 100 transactions/month)
  2. Growth Pattern: Transactions grow quarterly by your specified growth rate (e.g., 15% every 3 months)
  3. Revenue Per Transaction: Each transaction generates revenue based on your average order value (e.g., R3,000)

üîç Key Revenue Drivers:

  • Transaction Volume: More customers = more revenue
  • Growth Rate: Higher quarterly growth = faster revenue expansion
  • Order Value: Higher average transaction value = more revenue per customer

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Notes:

  • Revenue grows in quarterly steps, not monthly
  • The model assumes consistent growth throughout the 24-month period
  • All revenue is transaction-based - no subscription or recurring fees
  • Revenue calculations are independent of costs - they don't affect each other

üî¨ Technical Formula Details:

Monthly Revenue Calculation:

Revenue = Starting Transactions √ó (1 + Growth Rate)^Quarter √ó Average Order Value

Where:

  • Starting Transactions: Your baseline volume (e.g., 100/month)
  • Growth Rate: Quarterly growth percentage as decimal (e.g., 15% = 0.15)
  • Quarter: Which quarter you're in (Month 1-3 = Q0, Month 4-6 = Q1, etc.)
  • Average Order Value: Revenue per transaction in ZAR

Example for Month 7 (Q2):

Revenue = 100 √ó (1 + 0.15)¬≤ √ó R3,000 = 100 √ó 1.3225 √ó R3,000 = R396,750

Why Quarterly Growth?

  • Business growth typically happens in cycles, not continuously
  • Allows time for market penetration and customer acquisition
  • More realistic than monthly growth for planning purposes
  • Aligns with typical business planning and reporting cycles

Important: Total of all product mixes should equal 100%.

KhulaPay (Lay-By) - 40%

Product Type: Lay-by service where customers pay in installments before receiving goods.

Risk Profile: Low risk - no credit extended, goods held as security.

Revenue Stream: Platform fees only, no interest income.

Target Market: Budget-conscious consumers, retail partnerships.

SharePay (Social) - 15%

Product Type: Social lending platform connecting borrowers and lenders.

Risk Profile: Medium risk - depends on social network and credit assessment.

Revenue Stream: Service fees on successful loan facilitation.

Target Market: Community-based lending, peer-to-peer finance.

EasyNow (BNPL) - 30%

Product Type: Buy-now-pay-later service for immediate purchases.

Risk Profile: High risk - unsecured credit with potential for default.

Revenue Stream: Interest income on outstanding balances.

Target Market: Online shoppers, retail partnerships, younger demographics.

FlexLine (Credit) - 15%

Product Type: Revolving credit line with flexible borrowing.

Risk Profile: Highest risk - unsecured revolving credit.

Revenue Stream: Interest income on outstanding balances.

Target Market: Established customers, higher credit scores, larger amounts.

‚ö†Ô∏è Risk & Scaling

Purpose: These parameters help manage risk and ensure sustainable growth.

General Contingency (%)

Definition: General contingency buffer to cover unexpected expenses or revenue shortfalls.

Purpose: Provides financial cushion for operational risks and market uncertainties.

Typical Range: 3-10% depending on business maturity and risk tolerance.

Application: Applied to total operational costs as a safety margin.

Credit Loss Rate (%)

Definition: Percentage of total outstanding loans expected to default and result in loss.

Business Impact: Direct impact on profitability and capital requirements.

Typical Range: 2-8% depending on product mix and risk management.

Management: Controlled through credit scoring, risk assessment, and collections.

Working Capital Buffer (ZAR)

Definition: Working capital buffer to ensure sufficient funds for operational expenses.

Purpose: Covers cash flow gaps and unexpected operational costs.

Calculation: Based on monthly operational costs and cash flow cycles.

Typical Range: 2-6 months of operational costs depending on business model.

Fully Loaded Cost Multiplier

Definition: Multiplier applied to fully loaded costs to account for all expenses.

Components: Includes overhead, benefits, taxes, and indirect costs.

Typical Range: 1.2-1.4 depending on business structure and benefits.

Application: Applied to salary costs to reflect total employment costs.
